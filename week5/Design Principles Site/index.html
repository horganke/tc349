Principles of Web Design

  1. Good affordance: Tumblr.com

    Though very simple in layout and appearance has many implicit actions available through accordances. Just by looking you can see you can:

    • enter an email address,
    • enter a password,
    • forgot password help (question mark)
    • click Log in
    • click Sign up
    • see the Terms
    • See the Privacy link
    • click the Posted by user link, leads to profile
    • click the users image, leads to profile
    • enter in the search field
    • click the explore further topics link

  2. Bad Affordance: Chrome browser

    Navigation, information, featured items all chunked into sections and nicely laid out. Everything seems to be its own entity not running together making it easily to read and process.

  4. Bad signal to noise ratio: Slickdeals.com

    Kind of a lot going on this homepage. Information is all in front of you in similar sizes instead of chunked.

  5. Good Hick’s Law Example: Basecamp

    The main option you have is signing up for a free trial. There’s a few other available info links or the option to sign in, so whatever you’re there to do you’re going to make the decision very quickly

  6. Bad Hick’s Law Example: Overstock.com

    Overstock.com sheer amount of options is going to slow down your ability to make a decision.

  7. Good Occam’s Razor: The 1975 Site

    Website for a band I like, very clean and simple. Columns of posts much like a blog and a simple line of clear navigation at the top.

  9. Good modularity example: Hotels.com

    Everything on the page is a module. A module of photos, of the map, search fields, reviews, overview. All very easy to view as a whole or search for something individual.

  10. Bad modularity example: Snowfall (longform New York Times article)

    These longform articles that cascade all the way down the page in one constant scrolling stream are pretty to look at, but not exactly efficient in terms of modular design. People would more easily be able to just see the content they want to see in modules rather that scrolling down a whole page.
